The Myth of the “Retired TT Player”: Why You Can’t Retire from a Pixel Game

So, here’s the deal: I was scrolling through some random online banter when I came across someone who referred to themselves as a “retired TT player.” At first, I thought it was a joke, but nope, this person was dead serious. And honestly? That got me thinking—what does it even mean to be “retired” from a pixel game? Spoiler alert: It doesn’t mean anything. Let’s break it down.

The Whole “Retirement” Thing

When we hear the word “retired,” most of us picture someone who’s put in the work, done their time, and is now kicking back with a drink in hand, enjoying the fruits of their labor. We’re talking years of dedication, stress, and probably a few too many meetings that could’ve been emails. But when it comes to gaming, especially something as low-stakes as a random pixel game, “retirement” feels a bit... over the top.

A Pixel Game Isn’t a Career

Look, I get it—games can be fun, competitive, even a little addictive. But when you start throwing around words like “retired” for something that’s basically a hobby, it’s time for a reality check. This isn’t the NFL, where you’ve got a shelf full of trophies and a body full of injuries to show for your efforts. It’s more like saying you’ve “retired” from playing dodgeball in gym class or that you’re a “former” Uno champion because you haven’t touched the cards in years. It just doesn’t carry the same weight.

Moving On vs. Retiring

Here’s the thing: It’s totally cool if you’ve decided to step away from a game. Maybe you’ve found something new to sink your time into, or maybe you just got bored. That’s life. But do we really need to call it “retirement”? Why not just say you’ve moved on? There’s no need to make it sound like you’ve hung up your jersey and walked away from the game with a tear in your eye and a standing ovation behind you. You played a game, you had fun, and now you’re onto the next thing—simple as that.

The Bigger Picture

At the end of the day, labeling yourself as “retired” from a pixel game feels like trying to add some epic backstory to something that doesn’t need it. It’s like calling yourself a “former Pokémon master” because you haven’t touched your old Game Boy in years, or claiming you’ve “retired” from eating Pop-Tarts because you’re on a health kick. Just because you’re done with something doesn’t mean it needs a whole dramatic exit.

So, next time you’re about to call yourself a “retired” player, maybe think twice. Just say you’re done and move on. Save the word “retired” for when you’re actually kicking back in your golden years, reminiscing about the real grind. Until then, it’s just a game—no need to make it more than that.
